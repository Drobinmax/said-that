Roshi Bhadain said that he sees himself as a Mauritius

November 21, 2019 0 By Beverly Ma

The leader of the Reform Party has joined in the program “In the heart of the movement” in order to confirm that the party has given its voters the option of identifying themselves as belonging to the general population if they so wish.

Roshi Bhadain maintains that he sees himself as a Mauritius. It is for this reason, he says, that when he presented his application to the Philippe Rivalland Government School, he declared that he belonged to the general public.

When he appeared in a TV program, he said, “The executive committee of the Reform Party has left the choice to its candidates to identify themselves as part of the ‘general population’, because for us, it represents better Mauritians, unless some want to identify with a community precise.”

According to Roshi Bhadain, the Reform Party’s strategy to claiming membership of the general population is not a ploy to improve its chances of winning the Best Loser seat in District No. 20. The leader of the Reform Party emphasized that he positioned himself “to be chosen, not to be the best loser.” He said that he would win over Rajesh Bhagwan, the candidate of the MMM, in this electorate.

Besides Bhadain, then there was another guest of the program, Lindsey Collen de Lalit, a candidate for District No. 3. She clarified that Lalit had chosen to rely on a draw to award the community to each of her 24 candidates. “It’s a purely administrative procedure. We will not reveal the outcome of this draw, “says Lindsey Collen.  She also called for a proportional electoral overhaul that will incorporate the Best Loser System.
